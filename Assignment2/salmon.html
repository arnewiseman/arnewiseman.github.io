Chinook Salmon (Oncorhynchus tshawytscha)

Known as the largest species in the Pacific salmon genus, Chinook salmon are highly valued for their size and the quality of their meat.

Coho Salmon (Oncorhynchus kisutch)

Known for their aggressive behavior and popularity among sport fishermen, Coho salmon are distinguished by their bright silver sides and blue-green backs.

Chum Salmon (Oncorhynchus keta)

Also known as Dog Salmon, Chum salmon are recognized for their distinctive calico coloring during the breeding season.

Sockeye Salmon (Oncorhynchus nerka)

Renowned for their vibrant red color during spawning, Sockeye salmon are a key species in both commercial fisheries and native ecosystems.

Pink Salmon (Oncorhynchus gorbuscha)

Also known as Humpback salmon, Pink salmon are distinguished by their large dorsal humps when spawning.

Atlantic Salmon (Salmo salar)

Known for their remarkable life cycle, spanning freshwater and marine environments, Atlantic salmon are iconic fish revered for their history and taste.
