Coastrange Sculpin (Cottus aleuticus)

This species is known for its unique adaptation to cold, fast-moving streams and rivers in the Pacific Northwest.

Mottled Sculpin (Cottus bairdi)

Known for its camouflage abilities, the Mottled sculpin blends into its rocky freshwater habitat.

Paiute Sculpin (Cottus beldingii)

This species is notable for its adaptation to diverse freshwater environments.

Slimy Sculpin (Cottus cognatus)

A small, bottom-dwelling fish known for its slimy texture and ability to withstand cold temperatures.

Inland Riffle Sculpin (Cottus gulosus)

Known for its distinctive appearance and preference for fast-flowing streams.

Reticulate Sculpin (Cottus perplexus)

This species is distinguished by its unique reticulate (net-like) pattern, which camouflages it among river stones.

Torrent Sculpin (Cottus rhotheus)

A species known for dwelling in fast-flowing streams and rivers.
